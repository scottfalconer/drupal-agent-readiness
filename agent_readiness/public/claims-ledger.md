## Known Confounds And Caveats

- The stock Haven hidden paths in the headline observation are under `/admin`.
  A Drush-only agent can reject those paths because `/admin` is conventionally
  unsafe without identifying the disabled View. That is why the public result
  reports both verdict and reasoned metrics.
- The controlled non-admin hidden-path fixture exists as breadth evidence, but
  it needs a pinned repeated experiment before replacing the stock Haven case.
- The equipped condition changes module/tool availability and prompt guidance:
  its prompt names a fixture command that emits path state and normative advice.
  Retained equipped answers cite disabled declarations and judgments differ
  between conditions; the package does not establish which bundled component
  caused that association or prove model capability or resolver completeness.
- The headline alias-safety evidence is re-scoreable from retained artifacts,
  but the original Claude workflow is not a fully pinned replication package:
  the non-public harness, resolved model snapshots, budget, starting-site hash,
  and exact prompt-as-run provenance are not all independently captured outside
  the retained scratch workflow.
- The public tasks are curriculum as well as evidence. Future progress claims
  need fresh variants or pinned repeat conditions.
- The evaluator contract is task-specific. If the evaluator or rubric is wrong,
  that is a task failure, not an agent failure, and the claim computed from that
  rubric must be re-evaluated.

## Frontier Canary Boundary

The repository contains an adversarially tested canary implementation, but the
public package ships no canary run. Its registered task is schema-constrained
interpretation of a trusted-host snapshot. A future passing canary can validate
runner, sandbox, evidence-custody, and scoring mechanics for its exact two-slot
roster; it cannot establish direct Drupal discovery or operation, collector
truth, a treatment effect, or Drupal-side improvement.
